USD weakens → Gold USD rises, but Gold MYR may not
Gold is priced in USD. A stronger Ringgit offsets USD gold price moves — so local gold prices can stay flat even when global gold rallies. This is why gold fell from RM 651 → RM 583 in two weeks despite global gold holding firm.
🏦
BNM rate hold → Bond inflows → Stronger MYR
BNM holding at 2.75% while the Fed considers cuts narrows the rate gap in Malaysia's favour. Foreign bond investors pour in for yield — RM 16.5B YTD — strengthening MYR, lowering local gold prices and making imports cheaper.
🛢️
Iran conflict → Oil spike → Inflation → BNM caution
Higher oil boosts Petronas export revenue but raises domestic fuel costs, nudging CPI up. This limits BNM's room to cut rates — keeping rates elevated longer, attracting more foreign capital and indirectly supporting MYR.
📦
Strong MYR: winners and losers
A strong Ringgit helps importers (cheaper electronics, food, machinery) and data centre FDI projects. But it squeezes palm oil and semiconductor exporters whose USD revenues convert to less Ringgit at home.
